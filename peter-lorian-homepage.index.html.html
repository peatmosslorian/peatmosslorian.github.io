The person

Peter Lorian

They / He  ·  Mx. Lorian

peter.lorian@sd71.bc.ca 250.702.3178

I was born in Aotearoa (New Zealand) and grew up on the Stó:lō territories in the Fraser Valley. In 2016, I moved to the territories of the K'ómoks First Nation, where I live and teach. My family spends summers along the Puntledge River and our neighbourhood is our community.

As a TTOC with experience as an EA, youth support worker, and community educator, I've worked across Lake Trail Community School, Aspen Park Elementary, and district-wide support roles. I bring a strong foundation in learning differences, relational practice, and social justice pedagogy.

My Mennonite ancestry — grandparents from Mount Lehman, BC, shaped by ostracism and sheltering others — runs through my commitment to classrooms where everyone belongs.

Educational philosophy

Three commitments that shape my practice

People

Respectful, mutual relationships with learners, families, colleagues, and community. Learning is relational. I model vulnerability and create democratic classrooms where students have a voice.

Subject areas

A holistic, cross-curricular approach that integrates Indigenous knowledge and brings community into the classroom — and the classroom into the community.

Ways of learning

Universal Design for Learning as a foundation. Inquiry-based projects, whole-group dialogue, and student choice in how they demonstrate understanding. All students are intelligent.

Featured project

CTRL+ALT+MASC

Rewriting the rules for Gen Z/Alpha boys

How can creative, school-based interventions support boys in reimagining masculinity through empathy, equity, and connection — particularly in inclusive classrooms that serve neurodivergent and marginalized students? This resource curation draws on bell hooks, Michael Kimmel, WiseGuyz, White Ribbon, and current research on masculinist influencers in schools.
